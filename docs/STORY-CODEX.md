# StudyLah Legends — The Lightbearer Saga (Story Codex)

> Original IP for StudyLah. Copyright-free, Singapore-rooted, exam-prep at its
> heart. This is the narrative spine the game is designed around. Every wild
> monster is a real exam question wearing a costume; every "battle" is answered,
> not fought; every reward is effort, never a grade. Friendly-spooky tone
> ceiling — nothing "dies", the Fog just goes *quiet*, and beaten things
> *unclench* and sit down. Players are 13–17.

---

## THE WORLD IN ONE BREATH

The island of **Singapura** has a second weather no forecast will name: **the
Fog**. It is not water in the air — it is the cold hush that rolls in the night
before a paper, the voice that says *don't look, you'll only find out you can't*.
When a student stops facing questions, a little more Fog pools in the low ground.
Left long enough, it swallows whole neighbourhoods; the people inside don't
vanish, they just… stop trying, and are forgotten by everyone but the Fog.

A hundred and fifty years ago it took the **Old Campus** at one-north whole — a
cohort of the island's brightest, gone quiet in a single exam season. Since
then the island has been guarded by **beacons**: lamps that only light when a
student answers something honestly, out loud, unafraid. For a generation the
beacons have been dark. The **Fog Order** — the hush given hands and a hood —
made sure of it.

You are the first **Lightbearer** in a generation: a student who can walk into
the Fog, face the question inside a monster, and light the lamp anyway.

## THE CAST (all original)

- **You — the Lightbearer.** Chosen at onboarding as one of three researchers:
  - **JUN**, the steady all-rounder — first out the door every morning, maps the routes so no one gets lost.
  - **MEI**, quick and curious — keeps the neatest notebook on the island; the Fog hates a good index.
  - **AGENT X**, the transfer student nobody can place — their file is one page, and it just says *pass*.
- **Gugu**, your companion spirit — a small ghost with a lantern, traced from an old exam-hall nightlight. Gugu can't answer for you (spirits can't sit papers) but it can *hold the light steady* while you think, and it evolves with your effort: **Gugu → Guglow → Gugleam → Gubright → Guguardian**. It is the heart of the game's warmth; it cheers every strike and never once judges a wrong answer.
- **Elder Maple**, keeper of Haven Hollow — the retired first Lightbearer who lost her own cohort to the Old Campus and has been lighting one lamp a night ever since, waiting for a successor.
- **Kai**, your rival — races you across the island because his elder sister was in a cohort the Fog took; he's terrified that slowing down means it catches him too. His arc: learning that *facing* the question, even slowly, beats outrunning it.
- **Commander Murk**, the Fog Order's field commander — and the tragedy at the story's centre. He was **Maple's first prodigy**, the first beacon-keeper. One bad year, one exam season where everyone who'd praised him looked away, and he chose the quiet on purpose. He is not evil; he is a warning.
- **The Six Gurus** — masters of the six Provinces, each teaching one subject family, each with a lantern of their own: **Guru Fen** (Chemistry), **Guru Tor** (Physics), **Guru Vale** (Biology), **Guru Sum** (Mathematics), **Guru Mere** (Geography), **Guru Ash** (History/Humanities). They never test to rank you; they teach, then check.
- **The Three Hushes** — the Fog Order's lieutenants, each a way a student loses a paper they knew:
  - **The Whisper** — *doubt*. Hides one right answer behind fog on the page.
  - **The Hurry** — *panic*. Rushes the clock so you skip the reading.
  - **The Blank** — *freeze*. The white page, the mind gone quiet.
- **Rin the Duel Warden** — keeper of the honest duel: two students, one sealed set of questions, no audience.
- **The Examiner** — the silent final figure at the island's peak. Not a villain. The last, wordless test: can you sit the real thing, alone, with no beacon to light and no one watching?

## THE MAP — SINGAPURA (all real Singapore names)

The island is laid over the true map of Singapore. Home is the heartland; the
Fog pools at the edges and the forgotten places.

- **Haven Hollow — Toa Payoh** (home town). Elder Maple's study, the plaza fountain, the Academy, the Duel Hall, the four homes. The safe centre.
- **The Six Provinces** — one per subject you own, each a real heartland town: **Ang Mo Kio, Bishan, Tampines, Bedok, Jurong East, Woodlands…** Each Province has a route of wild questions, a town, a Guru, and a **Beacon Gym** to light.
- **The Undercroft** — beneath each Province, an echo-stone puzzle chamber where the subject's hardest ideas are kept.
- **Saltwind Steps — Marine Parade** (the coast). A mixed-subject breather shore, opens once two beacons burn; where the sea keeps the Fog thin.
- **The Summit of Clarity — Bukit Timah** (the island's true high point). The gauntlet: Commander Murk, then Kai, then the Examiner's hall.
- **The Old Campus — one-north** (post-game). The place the Fog took first; you go back to light the ninth, unofficial lamp and bring a lost cohort home.

### NEW EXPANSION ground (this saga adds)

- **The Sunken Cells — Fort Canning** (mid-game). Under the hill at the heart of the old town, the Fog Order's records-room, where Murk keeps the *names* of everyone the Fog ever hushed. Freeing a name relights a person.
- **The Lantern Walk — the Southern Islands / Lazarus** (late-game). A chain of small islets you cross by answering to keep your own lamp lit against the wind; the Whisper's home water.
- **The Reading Room — the old National Library / Bras Basah** (post-game hub). Where the Gurus gather once the island is safe; the calm New Game+ epilogue space (a campfire "THE END IS JUST NEW GAME+").

## THE STORY — FOUR ACTS (10× the stakes, same gentle tone)

### ACT I — THE LAMP THAT WOULDN'T LIGHT (Haven Hollow, Toa Payoh)
You wake in Haven Hollow the night before the season's first paper. Elder Maple
tells you the truth the island hides from children: the Fog is real, the beacons
are dark, and she is too old to light another. She offers you the choice of
**researcher** and a **companion spirit, Gugu**. A **Fog Order grunt** is already
in the plaza, offering the town its usual "mercy" — *a paper you never sit is a
paper you never fail.* You face him (two questions), light the plaza lamp, and
the Fog pulls back for the first time in years. Kai, your rival, sees it and
races off to be the one who lights the next.

**Absorbs:** onboarding (hero + companion pick), the intro Elder beat, the first
Fog grunt fight, `rival1`.

### ACT II — THE SIX PROVINCES (your subjects)
Each subject you own is a Province with a town, a route of wild questions, a
**Guru** who teaches, and a **Beacon Gym**. Clear a gym and a beacon lights over
Haven — the sky over Toa Payoh gets one lamp brighter each time. Along the way:
- **The Undercroft** of each Province holds the subject's hardest chamber (the echo-stone puzzle) and a keystone.
- **Rin's Duel Hall** opens for honest friend-vs-friend duels.
- **The weekly Fog Front** rolls onto your *weakest* subject's landmark — the island tells you where you're bleeding marks, and asks you to go light it.
- **The Three Hushes** begin to appear as Province mini-bosses: **the Whisper** (fog on the page), **the Hurry** (the rushing clock), **the Blank** (the white page).

**Absorbs:** the whole Province/route/gym/Undercroft system, the Gurus/Academy,
the Duel Hall, the Fog Front, the season-pack mini-bosses, notebook echoes.

### ACT III — THE SUNKEN CELLS (Fort Canning) [NEW]
Two beacons in, Elder Maple gives you the first real clue: the Fog Order isn't
snuffing lamps at random — it's *keeping a list*. Beneath Fort Canning, in the
**Sunken Cells**, Commander Murk keeps the names of everyone the island ever
hushed, his own at the top. Descend past the Three Hushes, reading names aloud
to relight them one by one (each is a small set-battle in a subject you own).
At the bottom you don't fight Murk — you *find* him, and learn who he was:
Maple's first prodigy, the first beacon-keeper, the boy the island forgot after
one bad season. He leaves before you can answer him. The Fog over the island
thins by half. **The Saltwind Steps open** so the Provinces can finally reach
one another.

**Absorbs & re-frames:** gives Commander Murk his backstory a full act *before*
the Summit, so the Summit payoff lands harder. Reuses the set-battle + echo
systems. Saltwind unlock becomes a story beat, not just a gym-count gate.

### ACT IV — THE SUMMIT OF CLARITY (Bukit Timah)
Light every beacon and the Summit gate opens. The gauntlet is a climb:
1. **Commander Murk** blocks the path — three questions, every subject. He isn't trying to beat you; he's trying to prove the quiet was right. You win not by being faster but by having *written down the mark you dropped and come back for it* — the one move he never learned. He stands aside. (`fog2`)
2. **Kai**, at the spot his sister's cohort fell, races you one last time — and loses, and laughs, and the sky doesn't fall. (`rival2`)
3. **The Examiner's Hall** — the championship gauntlet across all your subjects. (`championship`)

**Absorbs:** the entire existing Summit sequence, verbatim in spirit.

### EPILOGUE — THE OLD CAMPUS & THE READING ROOM (one-north / Bras Basah) [post-game]
With the island safe, you go back to the place the Fog took first — the **Old
Campus** at one-north — and light the **ninth, unofficial lamp** (`ninth`),
walking room by room through the fog that's held a lost cohort for 150 years,
bringing them home. Then the Gurus gather at the **Reading Room** for the calm
New Game+ space: *the end is just New Game+.* And somewhere on a quiet islet, if
you've earned the trust of all three Hushes, **the Examiner** sits for one last,
wordless paper — the Red-on-Mt.-Silver moment: no dialogue, just "…", and the
hardest questions the island has.

**Absorbs:** the existing Old Campus / ninth-lamp epilogue; adds the silent
final-boss beat and a warm New Game+ hub.

- Banned words: guaranteed, 100%, confirmed, leaked, insider, sure-win.
- Answers graded server-side; keys never reach the client pre-grade.
- 100% original IP; friendly-spooky ceiling — nothing dies, the Fog goes quiet.
- All place names are real Singapore locations.
