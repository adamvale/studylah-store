  - Wave: v = f lambda = 25 x 1.2 = 30 m/s; T = 1/f = 1/25 = 0.04 s (500 Hz x 0.66 m = 330 m/s). Read
    wavelength from a displacement-DISTANCE graph, period from a displacement-TIME graph.
  - Reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection, both from the NORMAL (i = 40 -> r = 40).
  - Snell: n = sin i / sin r; sin 45 / sin 28 = 0.707/0.469 = 1.5; sin 40 / sin 25.3 = 1.5; graph of
    sin i vs sin r has gradient n = 0.75/0.50 = 1.50. Entering glass, light bends TOWARDS the normal.
  - Critical angle: sin c = 1/n; n = 1.5 -> c = 42 degrees (41.8); n = 1.33 -> c = 49 degrees (48.8).
    TIR needs a denser-to-less-dense ray AND incidence greater than the critical angle; at c the
    refraction angle is 90 degrees.
  - Lens: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v (compute 1/f then take the reciprocal): u = 25.0, v = 75.0 -> 1/f = 0.0533,
    f = 18.8 cm; u = 30, v = 60 -> 1/f = 0.0500, f = 20.0 cm. Magnification m = v/u; a graph of m
    against v has gradient 1/f (G = 0.190 -> f = 5.3 cm). Displacement method f = (D^2 - d^2)/(4D) =
    (10000 - 3457)/400 = 16.4 cm. A real image is inverted.
