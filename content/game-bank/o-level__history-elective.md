```yaml
kind: sbq

how: "Write one inference, then support it: 'This source suggests X... I know this because it says \"quote\".' Two supported inferences usually beat five unsupported ones. Never simply copy out the source."
```


```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Purpose - why was this source produced?"
what: "Explain what the author wanted the AUDIENCE to think, feel or do as a result of seeing the source."
how: "Purpose = message + audience + intended effect + context. Use the provenance: 'Published in [year], when [context], so the cartoonist wanted British readers to believe... in order to...'. A purpose answer that never mentions the audience is only a message answer."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Reliability and provenance"
what: "Judge how far a source can be trusted as evidence about a stated issue."
how: "Ask WHO, WHEN, WHY and TO WHOM. Then argue both ways: reasons it may be trustworthy (eyewitness, private, no reason to lie, matches other evidence) and reasons for caution (official, censored, written long after, written to persuade). Never end with a bare 'so it is unreliable' - say what it is unreliable FOR."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Utility - how useful is this source?"
what: "Judge usefulness FOR A STATED PURPOSE, using both content and provenance."
how: "Say what the source is useful for ('it shows what the government wanted the public to believe'), what it is less useful for ('it cannot tell us what people actually thought'), and why - linking each judgement to the provenance. 'Biased, therefore useless' is a self-inflicted wound: bias is itself evidence."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Comparison of two sources"
what: "Compare two sources by CONTENT and by overall MESSAGE, not just by surface detail."
how: "Pair the points: 'Source A says \"...\" while Source B says \"...\", so they disagree about...'. Then compare the big picture: do they share an attitude even where the details differ? Finish with a clear statement of how far they agree, and explain the difference using their provenance."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Cross-referencing - does Source B prove Source A?"
what: "Test whether one source supports, corroborates or undermines the claims of another."
how: "Quote the specific claim in A, then the specific evidence in B that supports or contradicts it. Then evaluate: are the two sources INDEPENDENT? Two officials of the same government agreeing proves less than agreement between an official source and an eyewitness on the other side. End with 'so B partly proves A because...'."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Surprise - how surprised are you by this source?"
what: "Judge a source against what you KNOW about the period, not against your personal feelings."
how: "Answer both ways. Not surprised BECAUSE (contextual knowledge that fits it, plus its provenance: 'this is exactly what a government minister would say in 1938'). Surprised BECAUSE (knowledge that conflicts with it, or a provenance that makes the content unexpected). Every reason must be a FACT you know, then a link back to the source."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "The [8] assertion - how far do these sources support this view?"
what: "The final and most predictable sub-question: use ALL the sources plus your own knowledge to test a stated view."
how: "Group, do not list. Paragraph 1: sources that SUPPORT the view, with quotations. Paragraph 2: sources that do NOT. Paragraph 3: EVALUATE - which sources carry more weight, and why (provenance, reliability, cross-reference)? Then bring in own contextual knowledge and finish with an explicit judgement: 'The sources support the view to a limited extent, because...'. Never leave a source unused."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Using contextual knowledge"
what: "Bring in what you know of the period to explain, test and judge the sources."
how: "Contextual knowledge should DO something: explain why the source was produced ('this appeared weeks after the Munich Agreement'), test a claim ('the source says X, but in fact...'), or fill a gap the sources leave. Dumping a paragraph of unconnected facts earns nothing - every fact must be tied to a source."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "The 'explain why' causal paragraph"
what: "The building block of every essay: a paragraph that EXPLAINS a cause rather than describing an event."
how: "Point - Evidence - Explain - Link. State the reason, give precise supporting facts (names, dates, terms), then explain the CONSEQUENCE step by step ('this mattered because... which meant... so...'), and link back to the question. If your paragraph never uses 'because', 'which meant' or 'as a result', it is narrative, not explanation."
```

```yaml
kind: sbq
rung: "Balanced argument and judgement - the [10] essay"
what: "Every essay is: \"'[statement].' How far do you agree? Explain your answer.\""
how: "Plan two or three paragraphs that AGREE and two that consider OTHER factors, each Point-Evidence-Explain-Link. Then write a real conclusion that WEIGHS them: which factor was the most important, and why - was it a trigger, an underlying cause, or a condition that made the others possible?
```
