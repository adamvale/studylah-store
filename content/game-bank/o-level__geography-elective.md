```yaml
  The hierarchy runs residential unit, precinct, neighbourhood, town.
  Several precincts together form a neighbourhood, and several neighbourhoods together form a town.
```

```yaml
type: short
topic: "GE3.2 Climate Change"
```

```yaml
type: short
topic: "GE3.3 Climate Action"
stem: "Name the term for a natural store, such as a forest or the ocean, that absorbs more carbon than it releases."
accepted: ["carbon sink", "a carbon sink", "carbon sinks"]
marks: 1
misconception: carbon-sink-name
worked: |
  A carbon sink absorbs and stores more carbon than it releases, removing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.
  Forests store carbon in biomass and soil; oceans dissolve it in seawater and store it in marine organisms. Protecting existing sinks is a mitigation strategy, because clearing them releases the stored carbon and removes future absorption.
```

```yaml
type: short
topic: "GE4.1 Plate Tectonics"
stem: "Name the type of plate boundary at which two plates slide horizontally past each other, with crust being neither created nor destroyed."
accepted: ["transform", "transform boundary", "transform plate boundary", "conservative boundary"]
marks: 1
misconception: transform-boundary-name
worked: |
  At a transform boundary the plates move horizontally past one another along a fault.
  No new crust is created, as it is at a divergent boundary, and none is destroyed, as it is at a convergent boundary. Friction locks the plates until the stress is released suddenly, which is why transform boundaries produce frequent, shallow earthquakes but few volcanoes.
```

```yaml
type: short
topic: "GE4.2 Earthquakes and Volcanoes"
stem: "Name the process in which shaking causes loose, water-saturated soil to lose its strength and behave like a liquid, so that buildings tilt and sink."
accepted: ["liquefaction", "soil liquefaction"]
marks: 1
misconception: liquefaction-name
worked: |
  Shaking raises the water pressure between the grains of loose, saturated sediment and forces them apart, so the ground can no longer support a load.
  Buildings then tilt, sink or topple even when the structure itself is sound. This is why soil type is a factor in tectonic disaster risk.
```

```yaml
type: short
topic: "GE4.3 Disaster Risk Management"
stem: "In the disaster risk framework, name the term for the presence of people, buildings and assets in places where a hazard can occur."
accepted: ["exposure"]
marks: 1
misconception: exposure-name
worked: |
  Risk = hazard × vulnerability × exposure.
  Exposure is about location: how many people and how much property lie in the area the hazard can reach.
  Vulnerability, in contrast, is about how badly those exposed would be harmed. Land-use planning reduces exposure; hazard-resistant design reduces vulnerability.
```
