```yaml
  - "DEBIT: Cost of the asset sold (transferred from the asset account)"
  - "CREDIT: Accumulated depreciation on that asset (transferred from the accumulated depreciation account)"
  - "CREDIT: Proceeds of sale (Dr Bank / Dr Trade receivable)"
  - "Balancing figure on the CREDIT side = LOSS on disposal (to income statement)"
  - "Balancing figure on the DEBIT side = PROFIT on disposal (to income statement)"
note: "Profit or loss = proceeds − net book value. Never compare the proceeds with the original cost."
```

```yaml
kind: poa
name: "Suspense Account"
lines:
  - "Open it with the trial balance difference, on the SHORTER side"
  - "If debits are short: DEBIT suspense with the difference"
  - "If credits are short: CREDIT suspense with the difference"
  - "Post the correcting half of every one-sided or unequal error to the opposite side"
  - "When all such errors are corrected the account has no balance and closes"
note: "Only errors that unbalance the trial balance go through suspense. An error of principle or commission never touches it."
```

```yaml
kind: poa
name: "Allowance for Impairment of Trade Receivables Account"

  - "If the allowance must DECREASE: Dr Allowance; Cr Income statement (a reduction in the expense)"
```

```yaml
lines:
  - "Receipts column: the imprest (float) restored at the start of the period"
  - "Total payments column, then the analysis columns (postage, stationery, travel, cleaning, ledger accounts)"
  - "Total each analysis column and post the total to the debit of that expense account"
  - "Cash in hand at period end = Imprest − Total payments"
  - "Reimbursement cheque at period end = Total payments (this restores the float to the imprest)"
note: "The reimbursement always equals the amount SPENT, which brings the float back to its fixed level."
```

```yaml
kind: poa
name: "Effect of Errors on Profit — Correction Table"
lines:
  - "Column 1: the error | Column 2: increase / decrease / no effect on profit | Column 3: amount"
  - "Expense understated (e.g. accrual omitted) → profit overstated → correction DECREASES profit"
  - "Expense overstated (e.g. prepayment omitted) → profit understated → correction INCREASES profit"
  - "Revenue expenditure posted as capital expenditure → profit overstated → correction DECREASES profit"
  - "Capital expenditure posted as revenue expenditure → profit understated → correction INCREASES profit"
  - "Errors between two statement-of-financial-position accounts (e.g. wrong customer) → NO effect on profit"
note: "Always ask: did the error touch an income or an expense account? If not, the profit is unchanged."
```
